Statistics, Part III 2021/22

I took mainly statistics, machine learning and information theory courses for my Part III in Cambridge (2021/22). Below is a list of courses that interest me the most:

Modern Statistical Methods

by Rajen Shah

The course covers Ridge Regression, Representer Theorem (Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space), Support Vector Machine, Lasso, KKT condition, Conditional Independence Testing, Graphical Lasso, debiased Lasso and multiple testing.

Statistical Learning in Practice

by Randolf Altmeyer

Covers many different concepts: Generalised Linear Models, kNN, K-means clustering, Bayesian GLMs, Linear Mixed Models, Principal Component Analysis, Neural Network, Time Series

Topics in Satistical Theory

by Richard Samworth

Covers Concentration Inequalities, Kernel Density Estimation, Non-parametric Regression and Cubic Splines

Concentration Inequalities

by Varun Jog

Covers Chernoff-Cramer method, Efron Stein inequality, Entropy method, Bounded Differences inequality, Transportation method
